The CT has a 5 amp secondary rating. The value entered created a secondary current of which is above the CT 5 amps secondary current rating. If the CT is going to see this amount of current continously you must check the maufactures literature. A typical rating would be RF 2.0 at 30°C ambient with RF 1.5 at 55°C ambient. These statements mean that in a 30°C ambient, the CT will safely carry on a continuous basis 2 times the nameplate rating and at 55°C ambient, it will carry 1.5 times the nameplate rating.

×

This CT has a class of C100, which means it saturates when the voltage on its secondary terminals get above 100V. The secondary voltage would need to be which is above 100V. In saturation the CT can not build up more voltage to continue to push current accurately. Often during a fault, CT's have to push very high current in order to properly supply current to protective relaying. If the CT saturates then the relay will not be supplied correct current and my fasely operate or not operate at all. In this case the circuit burden was 5 ohms, which is very high. If the circuit burden was lower the CT could drive more current accurately. Therefore measuring circuit burden is an important test and understaning how it relates to CT saturation is also important.
